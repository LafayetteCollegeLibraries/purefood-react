These maps show the export flow of cottonseed oil from U.S. ports to the world from 1880 to 1900. The width of the arrows represents the quantity of oil from tens of thousands to hundreds of millions of pounds. Because customs records from the U.S. and foreign trade records from foreign countries were too numerous to include with individual arrows, the maps show regional flows rather than specific national ones. Also note that inconsistency in record keeping leads to inconsistent labels for the export locations (some are continents, some are regions, some are nations), so we have sought to provide as much consistency as possible.


Overall, the trade grew from two export cities in 1880 (New York and New Orleans) to nine in 1900 and from six global regional destinations to nine. The largest export stream went to Southern Europe across all twenty years (principally Italy and southern France). By 1900, northern European nations (The Netherlands, Belgium, and the UK) played a bigger part in the trade. Full data sets of the flow paths are here.
